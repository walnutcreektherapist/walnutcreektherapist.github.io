Treatment that targets the trauma, not just the talk

For PTSD, panic, and anxiety I draw on EMDR, the Flash technique, exposure therapy, Cognitive Processing Therapy, and CBT. EMDR helps your brain reprocess distressing memories so they lose their grip; Flash makes that gentler by lowering the intensity before we work with the memory at all. The point is to change how those experiences live in your body and mind, not only to discuss them.

I track your symptoms with standard measures so we can both see what's improving and adjust if it isn't. And because I work as an integrated clinician, I tend to notice what others have missed — undiagnosed ADHD, depression, even sleep apnea behind years of fatigue — and get you to the right care alongside the work we do together.

Common questions

Frequently asked

Do you offer EMDR therapy?
Yes. I'm trained in EMDR and use it to treat trauma, PTSD, anxiety, and panic, in person in Walnut Creek and by video across California.
What is the Flash technique?
Flash is a gentle, EMDR-based method that lowers the intensity of a distressing memory with far less distress in the moment, which makes it easier to then process. I use it alongside standard EMDR.
Is this available online?
Yes — individual therapy is available in person in downtown Walnut Creek or by secure video for anyone located in California.
What does therapy cost?
Individual sessions are $275 per clinical hour. Private-pay and out-of-network, with a superbill available for reimbursement and HSA/FSA cards accepted.
How long does treatment take?
We track your symptoms with standard measures and review progress regularly, so the work stays focused and you can see it moving rather than guessing.
