Often the first to spot it

I work with a lot of adults with ADHD, and I'm frequently the first person to recognize it. Most undiagnosed adults don't think "ADHD" — they think they're disorganized, unreliable, or quietly ashamed they can't hold it together the way everyone else seems to. It's badly underdiagnosed in adults, and especially in women and in people who've spent a lifetime compensating.

Here's how I work with it: I recognize the signs and refer you for proper assessment — I don't diagnose it myself — and then we do the work that helps. That means practical skills, repairing the relationships ADHD has strained, and unwinding the years of shame that tend to pile up. If you have a partner, this often pairs naturally with couples work, because ADHD is one of the most common hidden drivers of the same fight on repeat.
